<p><p>The biological organic fertilizers market comprises primarily two types: microorganisms and organic residues. Microorganism-based fertilizers utilize beneficial bacteria, fungi, and other microorganisms to enhance soil fertility and plant growth. They improve nutrient availability and soil structure, promoting a sustainable agricultural ecosystem. Organic residues involve the use of plant and animal waste, such as compost, manure, and crop residues, which enrich the soil with organic matter and nutrients. Both types contribute to eco-friendly farming practices and support soil health.</p></p>
