<p><p>The gynecological cancer drugs market comprises several key types, including alkylating agents, plant alkaloids, anthracyclines, and antitumor antibiotics. Alkylating agents work by damaging the DNA of cancer cells, hindering their ability to multiply. Plant alkaloids, derived from plants, inhibit mitosis, disrupting cell division. Anthracyclines are a class of drugs that interfere with DNA replication and repair. Antitumor antibiotics prevent cancer cell proliferation by inhibiting RNA synthesis. Together, these categories represent targeted therapeutic options for treating gynecological cancers.</p></p>
