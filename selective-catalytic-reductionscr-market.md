<p><p>Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) is a technology used to reduce nitrogen oxide emissions from engines and industrial processes. It involves using a catalyst and a reductant, typically ammonia or urea, to convert harmful NOx into harmless nitrogen and water. The NH3-SCR market utilizes ammonia directly, while the Urea-SCR market employs urea, which is converted to ammonia before reacting. Ammonia-SCR refers to systems that inject ammonia gas for reduction. These markets cater to automotive, industrial, and power generation applications focusing on environmental compliance and sustainability.</p></p>
