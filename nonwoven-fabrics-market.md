<p><p>Nonwoven fabrics are categorized into several market types based on their production methods. Dry-laid processes involve carding and bonding fibers without liquid, resulting in lightweight fabrics. Spunmelt combines spunlaid and meltblown techniques, producing strong, high-performance materials suitable for filtration and medical applications. Wet-laid uses water to integrate fibers, creating absorbent and dense structures commonly found in hygiene products. Other categories may include needle-punched and thermal bonded methods, offering diverse applications across industries like automotive, construction, and consumer goods.</p></p>
