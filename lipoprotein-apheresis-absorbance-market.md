Key types include cellulose gel, which utilizes a matrix made from cellulose to selectively absorb lipoproteins, and Sepharose, a gelatinous medium that offers high specificity and efficiency in lipoprotein separation. The choice between cellulose gel and Sepharose largely depends on the desired absorption characteristics and clinical requirements.</p></p>
