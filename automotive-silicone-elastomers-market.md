<p><ul><li>High Temperature Vulcanised (HTV)</li><li>Room Temperature Vulcanised (RTV)</li><li>Liquid Silicone Rubber (LSR)</li><li>Fluorosilicone Rubber (FSR)</li><li>High Consistency Silicone Rubber (HCR)</li><li>Silicone Gels</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive silicone elastomers market encompasses various types that cater to diverse application needs. High Temperature Vulcanised (HTV) silicone offers enhanced heat resistance, ideal for engine components. Room Temperature Vulcanised (RTV) silicone molds at ambient temperatures, useful for seals and gaskets. Liquid Silicone Rubber (LSR) allows for intricate designs and high-volume production. Fluorosilicone Rubber (FSR) provides excellent chemical resistance. High Consistency Rubber (HCR) is utilized for durable, solid applications, while silicone gels offer cushioning and vibration damping properties, enhancing vehicle performance and longevity.</p></p>
