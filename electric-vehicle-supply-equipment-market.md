<p><p>Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment (EVSE) encompasses two primary charging types: AC charging and DC charging. AC charging, typically using Level 1 or Level 2 chargers, converts alternating current from the grid into usable power for vehicles, making it suitable for home or workplace charging. DC charging, on the other hand, delivers direct current and offers faster charging times, primarily used in public charging stations. Both types are essential for supporting electric vehicle infrastructure, catering to varying consumer needs and charging speeds.</p></p>
