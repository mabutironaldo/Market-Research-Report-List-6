<p><p>Laboratory trinocular microscopes are categorized into two main types: inverted and upright. Inverted microscopes are designed for observing samples in culture dishes, making them ideal for biological research and cell cultures, as they allow light to pass from below. Upright microscopes, on the other hand, are used for examining solid or mounted specimens, providing a direct view from above. Both types feature a trinocular head for simultaneous image capture and display, catering to diverse laboratory needs and applications.</p></p>
