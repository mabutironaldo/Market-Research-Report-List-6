<p><p>Rotator cuff arthropathy repair involves various surgical options tailored to address shoulder dysfunction. Reverse total shoulder replacement is often used for patients with massive cuff tears and arthritis, enabling improved function by shifting the shoulder's center of rotation. Muscle/tendon transfer reestablishes stability by relocating healthy muscles to compensate for damaged rotator cuff muscles. Partial joint replacement involves replacing damaged parts of the shoulder while preserving healthy areas. Other niche surgical techniques may include arthroscopy and debridement, depending on individual patient needs.</p></p>
