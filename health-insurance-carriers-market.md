<p><p>Health insurance carriers can be categorized into several market types. Health Maintenance Organizations (HMOs) emphasize preventive care and require members to use a network of providers, often needing referrals for specialists. Preferred Provider Organizations (PPOs) offer more flexibility in choosing providers and donâ€™t require referrals, though using preferred providers reduces costs. Point-of-Service (POS) plans combine features of HMOs and PPOs, allowing members to choose between network and out-of-network services. Indemnity Benefit Plans provide more freedom in provider selection, reimbursing a percentage of costs after care.</p></p>
