<p><p>Air-electrode batteries leverage oxygen from the environment to facilitate energy storage and discharge, distinguishing them from traditional batteries. Zinc-air batteries utilize zinc as the anode and atmospheric oxygen as the cathode, offering high energy density and lightweight characteristics, making them suitable for hearing aids and electric vehicles. In contrast, lithium-air batteries, using lithium as the anode, promise even higher energy densities, appealing for electric vehicles and portable electronics. Both types are under research for scalability and efficiency improvements in renewable energy applications.</p></p>
