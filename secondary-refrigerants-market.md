<p><p>Secondary refrigerants are substances used to transfer heat from one location to another in refrigeration systems, acting as a medium that absorbs heat before transferring it to a primary refrigerant or directly to the environment. Common examples include water, glycol solutions, and brine. These agents enhance the efficiency and effectiveness of cooling systems by providing a more stable temperature control environment, making them essential in various applications such as commercial refrigeration, HVAC systems, and food storage.</p><p>The Secondary Refrigerants Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.6% during the forecast period, driven by increasing demand for energy-efficient cooling solutions and the expansion of industries requiring advanced refrigeration technologies. The trend towards sustainable and eco-friendly refrigerants serves to enhance market growth, further supported by regulations on the use of high-global warming potential gases. Additionally, the rising emphasis on food safety and preservation boosts the adoption of secondary refrigerants across the food and beverage sector. Innovations in formulations and applications will continue to propel the market, making it increasingly integral to modern refrigeration and air conditioning systems.</p></p>
