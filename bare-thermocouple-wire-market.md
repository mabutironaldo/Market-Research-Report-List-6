<p><p>The bare thermocouple wire market includes various types distinguished by their composition and temperature ranges. Type T (copper-constantan) is ideal for low temperatures. Type J (iron-constantan) operates well in oxidizing atmospheres, while Type E (chromel-constantan) offers high sensitivity. Type K (chromel-alumel) is widely used due to its versatility and broad range. Type N (nicrosil-nisil) provides stability at high temperatures. Type S (platinum-rhodium) and Type R (platinum-rhodium) are suited for high-temperature applications, particularly in scientific and industrial settings.</p></p>
