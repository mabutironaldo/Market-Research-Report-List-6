<p><p>The Polypropylene (PP) nonwoven fabric market includes three main types: meltblown, spunbonded, and staple fibers. Meltblown fabrics are produced by extruding molten polymer through a spinneret, creating fine fibers suitable for filtration and medical applications. Spunbonded fabrics, formed by bonding continuous filaments, are robust and widely used in hygiene products and construction. Staple fibers, made from shorter fiber lengths, offer versatility and durability, often used in upholstery, automotive, and apparel industries, meeting various functional needs.</p></p>
