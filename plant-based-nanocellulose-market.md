<p><p>The plant-based nanocellulose market primarily consists of two types: Nanofibrillated Cellulose (NFC) and Nanocrystalline Cellulose (NCC). NFC is produced through mechanical and chemical processes that yield flexible, fibrillar structures, making it ideal for applications in food, cosmetics, and packaging. NCC, on the other hand, consists of rigid crystalline particles obtained via acid hydrolysis, offering superior strength and stability, suitable for composites, pharmaceuticals, and biomedicals. Both types leverage cellulose's renewable properties for diverse industrial applications.</p></p>
