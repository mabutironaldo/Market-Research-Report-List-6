<p><p>The Racing Clutches market includes three primary types: Carbon/Carbon, Metallic, and Cerametallic. Carbon/Carbon clutches are lightweight, heat-resistant, and ideal for high-performance racing due to their durability and smooth engagement. Metallic clutches, made from sintered metals, offer high torque capacity and are robust for extreme conditions but can be harsh on drivetrains. Cerametallic clutches combine ceramic and metallic materials, balancing heat resistance, durability, and smoother engagement, making them suitable for both racing and high-stress applications. Each type caters to specific performance and durability needs in motorsports.</p></p>
