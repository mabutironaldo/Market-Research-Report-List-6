<p><p>Preclinical tomography systems are widely used in research settings for studying small animals. The three main types of preclinical tomography systems are CT (computed tomography), PET (positron emission tomography) & SPECT (single-photon emission computed tomography), and composite systems which combine multiple imaging modalities. CT systems provide detailed anatomical information, PET systems offer molecular and metabolic information, and SPECT systems provide functional and molecular imaging. Composite systems combine the strengths of different modalities to offer a more comprehensive view of biological processes in small animals.</p></p>
