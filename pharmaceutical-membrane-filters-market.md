<p><p>The pharmaceutical membrane filters market is categorized into microfiltration, ultrafiltration, nanofiltration, and others. Microfiltration is used for removing larger particles and bacteria, ensuring sterile filtration. Ultrafiltration operates at a smaller scale, separating macromolecules, proteins, and enzymes, crucial for drug formulation. Nanofiltration deals with even finer particles, selective ion removal, and softening processes. Other types may include reverse osmosis and tailored filtration solutions for specific applications, each catering to distinct filtration needs within the pharmaceutical industry.</p></p>
