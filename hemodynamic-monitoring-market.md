<p><p>The hemodynamic monitoring market encompasses three primary applications: minimally invasive, invasive, and non-invasive techniques. Minimally invasive methods involve using catheters or sensors inserted through small incisions to monitor heart function and blood flow with reduced patient risk. Invasive methods require direct access to the bloodstream or heart but provide detailed data. Non-invasive techniques, such as ultrasound or wrist monitors, assess hemodynamics without penetrating the skin, offering convenience and comfort, making them suitable for continuous monitoring across various clinical settings.</p></p>
